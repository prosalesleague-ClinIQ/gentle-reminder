# Privacy Policy - Gentle Reminder

**Effective Date:** April 1, 2026
**Last Updated:** April 1, 2026

---

## 1. Introduction

Gentle Reminder ("we," "our," or "the Platform") is a dementia care platform designed to support patients, caregivers, and families through cognitive exercises, medication management, behavioral monitoring, and personalized care tools. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, share, and protect your personal and health information.

---

## 2. Information We Collect

### 2.1 Patient Health Information

- **Cognitive Assessment Data:** Scores from orientation, identity, memory, language, executive function, and attention exercises, including response times and accuracy.
- **Session Data:** Session frequency, duration, completion status, and exercise results.
- **Medication Records:** Medication names, dosages, schedules, administration logs, and adherence tracking.
- **Behavioral and Biomarker Data:** Cognitive response delays, routine disruption patterns, sleep irregularity indicators, speech biomarkers, and movement patterns.
- **Medical History:** Diagnosis date, cognitive stage classification, and clinician notes.
- **Incident Reports:** Records of safety incidents including type, severity, description, witnesses, and actions taken.

### 2.2 Personal Information

- **Account Information:** Name, email address, date of birth, preferred name, city, timezone, and preferred language.
- **Family Member Information:** Names, relationships, photos, voice messages, and video messages provided for therapeutic use.
- **Photos and Media:** Patient photos, voice recordings, story transcriptions, and memory items uploaded by patients or family members.

### 2.3 Technical Information

- **Usage Data:** Feature usage patterns, session timestamps, and engagement metrics.
- **Device Information:** Device type and operating system (used for platform compatibility only).
- **Location Data:** Location information from wearable devices (when enabled and consented to) for safety monitoring and geofencing.

---

## 3. How We Use Your Information

### 3.1 Clinical Care

- Delivering personalized cognitive exercises adapted to the patient's cognitive stage and performance history.
- Tracking medication schedules and sending reminders.
- Generating daily and weekly reports for care team review.
- Monitoring behavioral patterns and generating alerts for potential cognitive changes.
- Calculating biomarker scores and risk predictions to support clinical decision-making.

### 3.2 Caregiver and Family Support

- Providing caregivers with dashboards showing patient progress and care metrics.
- Enabling family members to contribute photos, voice messages, and stories for therapeutic activities.
- Sending notifications about patient status changes, medication adherence, and alerts.

### 3.3 Platform Improvement

- Analyzing aggregated, de-identified usage data to improve exercise effectiveness and user experience.
- Monitoring system performance and reliability.

### 3.4 Research (With Consent Only)

- With explicit, separate consent, de-identified data may be used for dementia care research.
- Research participation is entirely voluntary and does not affect care quality.
- Research data is always de-identified before use, removing all direct identifiers.

---

## 4. How We Share Your Information

### 4.1 Authorized Care Team

Patient data is shared only with authorized members of the care team:

- **Assigned Caregivers:** Access patient data for their assigned patients only.
- **Clinicians:** Access clinical data for patients under their care.
- **Family Members:** Access limited data for their linked patient, as configured by the care team.
- **Facility Administrators:** Access aggregate facility data and manage user accounts.

### 4.2 Service Providers

We share limited data with service providers who help operate the platform, under Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) that require HIPAA-compliant data handling:

- Cloud infrastructure providers (data hosting and storage).
- Voice synthesis providers (for familiar voice features).
- Notification delivery services.

### 4.3 De-Identified Research

With separate consent, de-identified data (with all direct and indirect identifiers removed) may be shared with qualified research institutions studying dementia care and cognitive health.

### 4.4 Legal Requirements

We may disclose information when required by law, regulation, legal process, or governmental request.

### 4.5 Information We Never Share

- We never sell patient data to third parties.
- We never share identifiable health information for marketing purposes.
- We never provide data to insurance companies without explicit patient authorization.

## 5. Patient Rights

Under HIPAA and applicable state laws, patients (or their authorized representatives) have the following rights:

### 5.1 Right to Access

You may request a copy of your health information maintained by the Platform. We will respond within 30 days of receiving your request.

### 5.2 Right to Correction

You may request corrections to inaccurate or incomplete health information. We will respond within 60 days and either make the correction or provide a written explanation of why the correction was denied.

### 5.3 Right to Deletion

You may request deletion of your health information, subject to legal retention requirements. Clinical records may be retained for the minimum period required by applicable law even after a deletion request.

### 5.4 Right to an Accounting of Disclosures

You may request a list of disclosures of your health information made by the Platform for purposes other than treatment, payment, or healthcare operations.

### 5.5 Right to Restrict Uses and Disclosures

You may request restrictions on how your health information is used or disclosed. While we are not required to agree to all restrictions, we will honor reasonable requests where feasible.

### 5.6 Right to Confidential Communications

You may request that we communicate with you through specific channels or at specific locations.

### 5.7 Right to Receive Breach Notification

You will be notified within 60 days if a breach of your unsecured health information is discovered.

---

## 6. Data Retention

- **Clinical Records:** Retained for a minimum of 7 years after the last patient interaction, or longer if required by applicable state law.
- **Account Information:** Retained for the duration of the account relationship plus the applicable retention period.
- **Audit Logs:** Retained for a minimum of 6 years.
- **De-Identified Research Data:** May be retained indefinitely as it contains no identifiable information.
- **Configurable Retention:** Facility administrators can configure retention periods that meet or exceed regulatory minimums.

Upon expiration of the retention period, data is securely and permanently deleted.

---

## 7. Security Measures

We implement comprehensive security measures to protect your information:

- **Encryption:** All data is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256).
- **Access Controls:** Role-based access ensures users only see data necessary for their role.
- **Authentication:** Secure JWT-based authentication with configurable session timeouts.
- **Multi-Factor Authentication:** Required for clinical and administrative accounts.
- **Audit Logging:** All data access is logged and monitored.
- **Network Security:** Application infrastructure runs in isolated virtual private cloud environments with restricted network access.
- **Regular Assessments:** Annual security risk assessments and penetration testing.
- **Incident Response:** Documented incident response procedures with defined escalation paths.

---

## 8. Children's Privacy

Gentle Reminder is designed for adult patients and their care teams. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under the age of 13.
