# PROJECT SUMMARY / ABSTRACT

*(30-line limit per NIH SF424 instructions. ~2800 characters maximum.)*

---

Over 55 million people live with dementia globally. Standard cognitive assessment tools (MMSE, MoCA, ADAS-Cog) produce pass/fail feedback that triggers anxiety, agitation, and session abandonment in dementia patients — a documented clinical problem with no commercial solution. Caregivers lack real-time digital biomarkers; clinicians lack longitudinal decline-tracking tools; pharmaceutical trials for anti-amyloid therapies (Leqembi, Kisunla) lack scalable digital endpoints. We have developed Gentle Reminder, a clinical-grade software-as-a-medical-device platform with 23 novel algorithmic innovations spanning cognitive assessment scoring, digital biomarkers, multimodal state detection, and FDA SaMD compliance. The core innovation — a three-state positive-only feedback system (CELEBRATED / GUIDED / SUPPORTED) — architecturally guarantees no negative feedback reaches the patient while preserving granular performance data for clinicians. Additional innovations include dementia-adapted spaced repetition, asymmetric adaptive difficulty targeting a 70-85% comfort zone, multimodal cognitive state classification, and a composite digital biomarker engine. The platform is production-ready (53,000+ lines of code, 5 deployed applications, 10 languages, FHIR R4 integration) with 23 USPTO provisional patents filed and full FDA SaMD documentation complete (IEC 62304, ISO 14971 FMEA, QMS, 21 CFR Part 11, STRIDE cybersecurity). This Phase I project will clinically validate Gentle Reminder through three aims: (1) validate the Gentle Feedback Scoring System against MMSE, MoCA, and ADAS-Cog in a 60-patient cohort with target Pearson r ≥ 0.80; (2) characterize composite digital biomarker correlation with 6-month cognitive decline (target Pearson r ≥ 0.65); and (3) demonstrate caregiver burden reduction via Zarit Burden Interview (target ≥20% reduction). Successful validation enables FDA 510(k) submission, pharmaceutical clinical trial deployment, and Medicare reimbursement pathways. The 23-patent IP portfolio, production-ready platform, and comprehensive FDA SaMD documentation position Gentle Reminder for rapid Phase II expansion and commercial launch. Partnerships with NIH-funded memory centers ensure scientific rigor. Upon successful Phase I completion, Phase II will expand to multi-site validation, FDA Pre-Submission meeting, and real-world evidence collection to support commercial launch and payer reimbursement.
