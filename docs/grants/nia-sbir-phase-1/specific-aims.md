# SPECIFIC AIMS

---

Over 55 million people live with dementia globally, projected to reach 139 million by 2050¹. Existing cognitive assessment tools — the Mini-Mental State Examination (MMSE), Montreal Cognitive Assessment (MoCA), and Alzheimer's Disease Assessment Scale (ADAS-Cog) — produce pass/fail feedback that triggers documented anxiety, agitation, and session abandonment in dementia patients². Clinical literature establishes that negative feedback impairs subsequent learning and engagement in this population (Clare 2003; Bahar-Fuchs, Clare & Woods 2013, Cochrane Review). Yet these instruments remain standard-of-care because no alternative has both clinical validity AND dementia-safe delivery. Caregivers lack real-time digital biomarkers; clinicians lack longitudinal decline-tracking tools; pharmaceutical trials lack scalable, reproducible digital endpoints for anti-amyloid therapies (Leqembi, Kisunla).

**We have developed Gentle Reminder**, a clinical-grade software-as-a-medical-device (SaMD) platform with 23 novel algorithmic innovations spanning cognitive assessment scoring, digital biomarkers, multimodal state detection, and FDA SaMD compliance. Our core innovation — a **three-state positive-only feedback system** — architecturally guarantees that no negative feedback reaches the patient while preserving granular performance data for clinicians. The platform is production-ready: 53,000+ lines of code, 5 deployed applications, 10-language support, FHIR R4 integration. Twenty-three USPTO provisional patent applications have been filed securing priority dates across the algorithm portfolio.

**The objective of this Phase I application** is to clinically validate the Gentle Reminder cognitive assessment and digital biomarker platform against standard-of-care instruments in a prospective cohort of patients with mild-to-moderate Alzheimer's disease. Our **central hypothesis** is that Gentle Reminder produces cognitive assessment data equivalent to MMSE/MoCA/ADAS-Cog while dramatically reducing patient-reported anxiety and increasing session completion rates.

We will pursue this through three Specific Aims:

**Specific Aim 1: Validate the Gentle Feedback Scoring System against MMSE, MoCA, and ADAS-Cog.** In a 60-patient cohort across two memory clinics (N=30 mild AD, N=30 moderate AD), patients will complete both Gentle Reminder sessions and standard instruments over 12 weeks. We hypothesize our composite cognitive score will correlate at Pearson r ≥ 0.80 with MMSE and MoCA, and that State-Trait Anxiety Inventory (STAI) scores will decrease by ≥30% during Gentle Reminder sessions compared to standard testing. *Primary endpoint:* concurrent validity coefficient. *Secondary endpoints:* STAI score change, session completion rates.

**Specific Aim 2: Characterize digital biomarker correlation with 6-month cognitive decline.** Using our composite digital biomarker engine (speech hesitation, response time, sleep irregularity, routine disruption, medication adherence), we will track 60 patients for 6 months. We hypothesize baseline composite biomarker scores predict 6-month MMSE change with Pearson r ≥ 0.65. *Primary endpoint:* predictive validity of baseline biomarker vs. 6-month MMSE change. *Secondary endpoints:* per-biomarker predictive value.

**Specific Aim 3: Demonstrate caregiver burden reduction.** Primary caregivers of enrolled patients will complete the Zarit Burden Interview (ZBI) at baseline and 3 months. We hypothesize ZBI scores will decrease by ≥20% among caregivers using platform features. *Primary endpoint:* ZBI score change. *Secondary endpoints:* caregiver-rated patient engagement, facility census retention.

**Significance of Phase I:** Successful validation establishes Gentle Reminder as a clinically valid, dementia-safe alternative to standard cognitive instruments — enabling downstream FDA 510(k) submission, pharmaceutical clinical trial deployment as a digital endpoint for anti-amyloid therapies, and Medicare reimbursement pathways. Our 23-patent IP portfolio, FDA SaMD compliance documentation (IEC 62304, ISO 14971, QMS, 21 CFR Part 11), and production-ready platform position Gentle Reminder for rapid Phase II expansion and commercial launch upon successful validation.
