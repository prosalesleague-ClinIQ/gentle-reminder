Over 55 million people live with dementia globally, with 10 million new cases each year. Despite the recent approval of anti-amyloid therapies (Leqembi 2023, Kisunla 2024), clinical care depends on cognitive assessment instruments — the Mini-Mental State Examination (MMSE), Montreal Cognitive Assessment (MoCA), and Alzheimer's Disease Assessment Scale (ADAS-Cog) — that were designed decades ago. These instruments produce pass/fail feedback that triggers documented anxiety, agitation, and session abandonment in dementia populations (Clare 2003; Bahar-Fuchs, Clare & Woods 2013 Cochrane Review). Meanwhile, longitudinal cognitive decline tracking requires weeks of clinician time per patient, creating scale barriers to real-world evidence collection for the anti-amyloid class.

**We have developed Gentle Reminder**, a clinical-grade software-as-a-medical-device (SaMD) platform comprising 23 patent-pending innovations spanning cognitive assessment scoring, digital biomarkers, and multimodal cognitive state detection. Our central innovation — a **three-state positive-only feedback system (CELEBRATED / GUIDED / SUPPORTED)** — architecturally guarantees that no negative feedback reaches the patient while preserving granular performance data for clinicians. Additional innovations include dementia-adapted spaced repetition (modified SM-2), asymmetric adaptive difficulty targeting a 70-85% success zone, and a 5-analyzer composite digital biomarker engine combining speech hesitation, response time, sleep irregularity, routine disruption, and medication adherence. The platform is production-ready: 53,000+ lines of code, 5 deployed applications, FHIR R4 integration, 10-language support. Full FDA SaMD documentation is complete (IEC 62304, ISO 14971 FMEA, ISO 13485 QMS, 21 CFR Part 11, STRIDE cybersecurity).

**The long-term objective** of this research is to establish Gentle Reminder as the first dementia-safe cognitive assessment and longitudinal digital biomarker platform suitable for clinical care, pharmaceutical trial endpoints, and payer decision support. **The objective of this 3-year BrightFocus award** is to rigorously validate the platform in a 200-patient longitudinal cohort of mild-to-moderate Alzheimer's patients. Our **central hypothesis** is that Gentle Reminder produces cognitive assessment and biomarker data equivalent to gold-standard instruments while dramatically reducing patient-reported anxiety and enabling high-frequency monitoring impractical with traditional tools.

**Specific Aim 1: Validate cross-sectional concurrent validity.** In a 200-patient cohort (N=100 mild AD, N=100 moderate AD) recruited across two academic memory centers, participants will complete paired Gentle Reminder sessions + MMSE, MoCA, and ADAS-Cog-13 at baseline. *Hypothesis:* Gentle Reminder composite score correlates at Pearson r ≥ 0.80 with MMSE and MoCA. *Primary endpoint:* concurrent validity coefficient. *Secondary:* State-Trait Anxiety Inventory comparison, session completion rates.

**Specific Aim 2: Characterize longitudinal predictive validity.** Over 24 months, we will track 150 enrolled patients with monthly Gentle Reminder sessions and biannual standard assessments. *Hypothesis:* Baseline composite digital biomarker scores predict 24-month MMSE change with Pearson r ≥ 0.65, and monthly biomarker trajectories detect decline acceleration ≥3 months earlier than standard assessments. *Primary endpoint:* predictive validity coefficient. *Secondary:* time-to-decline-detection difference.

**Specific Aim 3: Establish real-world feasibility and caregiver impact.** Primary caregivers of enrolled patients will complete the Zarit Burden Interview (ZBI) at baseline, 6, 12, 18, and 24 months. *Hypothesis:* Caregivers of platform users show ≥20% ZBI reduction vs. non-user controls. *Primary endpoint:* ZBI score change. *Secondary:* facility census retention, platform feature utilization.

**Significance:** Successful validation establishes Gentle Reminder as clinically valid for FDA 510(k) submission (predicate K201738 Linus Health identified), enables pharmaceutical partners to adopt our platform as a scalable Phase IV / real-world evidence digital endpoint for anti-amyloid therapies, and creates the foundation for Medicare reimbursement. Within the BrightFocus mission, this grant advances the field by providing the first architecturally dementia-safe cognitive assessment platform to supplement or replace inadequate standard instruments.
