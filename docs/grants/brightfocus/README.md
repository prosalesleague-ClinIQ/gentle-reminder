# BrightFocus Foundation — Alzheimer's Disease Research Grant

**Program:** BrightFocus Alzheimer's Disease Research (ADR) Program
**Funder:** BrightFocus Foundation
**Award:** $300,000 over 3 years ($100K/year)
**Deadline:** Annually — typically November (Letter of Intent due ~Oct; full proposal ~Dec)
**Eligibility:** US-based research; junior investigators strongly preferred; academic-industry partnerships encouraged
**URL:** https://www.brightfocus.org/alzheimers/grants

## Strategic Fit

BrightFocus specifically funds:
- Early-stage translational research that may not be NIH-ready
- Novel biomarker and diagnostic development
- Early commercialization of promising technologies

Gentle Reminder's three-state positive-only feedback system and composite digital biomarker engine fit BrightFocus's "innovative diagnostic/monitoring tools for AD" priority.

Advantage over NIH SBIR: BrightFocus moves faster (6-9 months from submission to funding vs. NIH's 9-12), does not require extensive prior publication record, and accepts more exploratory research.

## Application Components

### 1. Letter of Intent (LOI) — Due Oct ~15
- 1-2 pages
- Hypothesis, aims, significance, investigator qualifications, budget summary
- See [specific-aims.md](specific-aims.md) for draft

### 2. Full Proposal — Due Dec ~15 (if LOI invited)
- 10 pages maximum scientific content
- Hypothesis + specific aims (1 page)
- Significance and innovation (2 pages)
- Research design and methods (5 pages)
- Preliminary data and rigor (1 page)
- Budget and justification (1 page)
- Biosketch (separate, NIH format)
- Letters of support (separate)

### 3. Required Forms
- Principal Investigator CV (NIH biosketch acceptable)
- Budget narrative
- Facilities & other resources
- Scientific abstract (500 words)
- Lay abstract (400 words)

## Reuses From SBIR Application

~70% of SBIR Phase I content reuses directly:
- Scientific rationale and background
- Three specific aims (modified for 3-year scope vs 6-month)
- Clinical validation methodology
- Biostatistical analysis plan
- Team + academic PI identification

**Key differences from SBIR:**
- Longer timeline (3 years vs 6 months Phase I)
- Larger cohort feasible (150-200 patients vs 60)
- Multi-year follow-up for progression analysis (not just cross-sectional)
- Lower commercial content required
- Junior investigator preference (works in our favor)

## Submission Strategy

### Recommended Timing
Submit LOI in Year 1 of operations (Oct cycle) even if SBIR is pending; grants are not mutually exclusive.

### Academic Partnership
Same academic PI identified for SBIR can lead BrightFocus grant. Subaward structure similar.

### Likelihood Assessment
- BrightFocus funds ~20-25 ADR grants per cycle out of ~300 applications
- ~7-8% funding rate
- Our ratio of "mature platform + novel algorithmic contribution" is favorable
- Strong if: (a) we secure a Tier-1 academic PI, (b) preliminary feasibility data from an existing deployment

## Letters of Support Strategy

Same template as SBIR (see [../nia-sbir-phase-1/letters-of-support-template.md](../nia-sbir-phase-1/letters-of-support-template.md)).

For BrightFocus specifically:
1. Clinical collaborator (from one of: UCSF MAC, MGH, Emory ADRC)
2. Institutional commitment letter (from the department chair or center director)
3. Industry partnership letter (optional; strengthens commercialization signal)
