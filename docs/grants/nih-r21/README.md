# NIH R21 — Exploratory/Developmental Research Grant

**Program:** R21 (Exploratory/Developmental Grant)
**Funder:** NIH / NIA (PA-22-195 Parent R21 or NIA-specific FOAs)
**Award:** Up to $275K direct costs over 2 years (combined)
**Deadlines:** Feb 16, Jun 16, Oct 16 (standard parent R21 deadlines)
**Eligibility:** US investigators; novel/innovative research requiring limited preliminary data
**URL:** https://grants.nih.gov/funding/activity-codes/r21

## Why R21 (vs. SBIR Phase I)

R21 is designed for **exploratory, early-stage research that does not require extensive preliminary data**. Unlike SBIR:
- R21 has NO commercialization component — purely scientific
- Can support mechanistic, hypothesis-generating work
- Academic PI leads (we would be subawardee or partner)
- Lower total budget but faster review cycle

**Our R21 angle:** Mechanistic biomarker discovery research — understanding the relationship between multimodal digital signals and underlying neurodegenerative pathology. Complementary to SBIR (clinical validation) and BrightFocus (longitudinal tracking).

## Strategic Fit — "AI/ML Biomarker Mechanism" Angle

R21 reviewers reward:
- Novel mechanistic hypotheses
- Innovative methodology
- Potential for transformative impact

Our R21 hypothesis: **The multimodal biomarker signal patterns detected by Gentle Reminder reflect specific underlying neurodegenerative pathophysiology, allowing the platform to identify AD subtypes (typical, LBD-associated, vascular, FTD) through digital signal signatures alone.**

This is mechanistic neuroscience, not just validation.

## Key Difference from SBIR

| Dimension | SBIR Phase I | R21 |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| Max award | $295K (FY25) | $275K direct / $400-450K total |
| Duration | 6-12 mo | 2 years |
| PI requirement | Small business PI | Academic PI typically |
| Commercialization | Required (20% of score) | Not required |
| Preliminary data | Preferred, not required | Not required |
| Success rate | 15-25% | 10-18% |

**Best combination:** File SBIR Phase I AND R21 in same cycle. They address different aspects (commercialization vs. mechanism) and don't compete against each other.

## Submission Strategy

### Option A: File with SBIR Phase I simultaneously (Feb 16 / Jun 16 / Oct 16 if SBIR is Sep 5 / Jan 5 / Apr 5)
Two proposals in parallel. Different PI + different scope. Total potential: $550K combined ($275K R21 + $275K SBIR I).

### Option B: File R21 alone
If SBIR is delayed or strategy shifts. R21 has lower barrier to entry (no commercial plan).

### Option C: File R21 as "bridge" while SBIR Phase I is under review
R21 review cycle is often shorter (3-4 months to first review) than SBIR (4-6 months). R21 award can provide funding during SBIR review period.
