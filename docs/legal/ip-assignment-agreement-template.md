# Founder Invention Assignment Agreement — Template

**Document ID:** FIAA-001
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date target:** Day the Delaware C-Corp is filed
**Status:** TEMPLATE — execute on entity-formation day. **Critical** — without this, the corp does not own the IP the founders built pre-entity, and the company has no value.

---

## Why this is non-negotiable

Every investor diligence will ask: *"Does the company own all the IP?"*

If the founder conceived or reduced to practice IP **before** the entity existed (e.g., during the 2025–2026 pre-entity work on Gentle Reminder), that IP is owned personally by the founder unless and until an assignment is executed. Without assignment:

- The corp does not own any of the 23 provisional patents
- The corp does not own the software platform (53K+ LOC)
- The corp does not own the trademark application
- Investors walk away; the round dies

This agreement assigns all pre-existing and ongoing IP to the corp.

## When to execute

- **Same day as entity formation.** Filed before 5pm local time on the day the Certificate of Incorporation issues.
- **Before** any investor conversation that would trigger diligence (i.e., before Tier 2 data room access is granted)
- **Before** any first stock issuance to the founder (so the assignment is simultaneous with the capital structure)
- **Before** any 83(b) election filing (the 83(b) covers founder stock; the FIAA covers the IP the stock represents)

## Required co-execution

- Founder (Christo Mack) signs as assignor
- Company (Gentle Reminder, Inc.) signs as assignee via its first authorized officer
- Witnessed or notarized per counsel guidance (Delaware does not require notarization for IP assignments, but some investors prefer it)

## Filing and recording

- **USPTO Patent Assignments:** record each provisional/non-provisional patent assignment at USPTO Assignment Recordation (Form PTO-1595 or Electronic Patent Assignment System). Fee ~$40 per document. Not legally required for validity but VERY strongly recommended for notice.
- **USPTO Trademark Assignments:** same database, same mechanism, same fee.
- **Copyright Office:** optional; most software copyright assignments are not filed, but the agreement itself is sufficient for enforceability.

## 3. ASSIGNMENT OF PRE-EXISTING IP

Founder hereby irrevocably assigns, conveys, and transfers to Company all right, title, and interest, throughout the world, in and to:

- All inventions conceived or first actually reduced to practice by Founder prior to the Effective Date relating to the Business, whether or not patentable, including without limitation the inventions listed in **Exhibit A** (the "Pre-Existing Inventions")
- All provisional patent applications, non-provisional patent applications, and patents issuing therefrom, whether filed before or after the Effective Date, that relate to the Pre-Existing Inventions
- All trademarks, service marks, trade dress, domain names, and brand elements relating to the Business, including without limitation those listed in **Exhibit B** ("Pre-Existing Trademarks")
- All copyrights in software, source code, documentation, user interfaces, and other works of authorship authored by Founder prior to the Effective Date relating to the Business, including those listed in **Exhibit C** ("Pre-Existing Copyrighted Works")
- All trade secrets, know-how, algorithms, parameters, weights, thresholds, dictionaries, and other confidential information relating to the Business developed by Founder prior to the Effective Date
- All databases and data collections relating to the Business

collectively, the "Pre-Existing IP."

This assignment is effective as of the date the Pre-Existing IP was first created, whether before or after the Effective Date, and is self-executing to the maximum extent permitted by law.

## 4. ASSIGNMENT OF FUTURE IP

Founder agrees that all inventions, works of authorship, software, trademarks, trade secrets, and other intellectual property conceived, developed, or authored by Founder during Founder's engagement with Company that:

(a) relate to the Business, current or prospective, of Company, or
(b) result from or are suggested by any work performed by Founder for Company, or
(c) are developed using Company's equipment, supplies, facilities, or Confidential Information

shall be "Assigned IP." Founder hereby irrevocably assigns all right, title, and interest in and to all Assigned IP to Company upon creation.

## 5. EXCLUDED IP

Founder retains ownership of intellectual property that:

(a) was conceived and reduced to practice entirely before Founder's engagement with Company,
(b) does not relate to the Business, and
(c) is listed in **Exhibit D** ("Excluded IP")

If no Excluded IP is listed in Exhibit D, Founder represents that there is no Excluded IP.

California Labor Code § 2870 notice (if applicable): This Agreement does not apply to any invention which qualifies fully under § 2870 of the California Labor Code — i.e., an invention developed entirely on one's own time, without using the employer's equipment, supplies, facilities, or trade secret information, and which either (a) does not relate at the time of conception or reduction to practice to the employer's business or actual or demonstrably anticipated research or development, or (b) does not result from any work performed for the employer.

## 6. FOUNDER OBLIGATIONS

Founder shall:

(a) promptly disclose to Company all Assigned IP;
(b) execute, at Company's request and expense, any documents necessary to perfect or record Company's title to the Assigned IP, including U.S. and foreign patent and trademark applications, assignments, and power-of-attorney forms;
(c) assist Company in prosecuting, maintaining, and enforcing the Assigned IP;
(d) not assign, license, encumber, or otherwise dispose of any Assigned IP to any third party without Company's prior written consent.

If Company is unable for any reason to secure Founder's signature on any document required to perfect Company's rights, Founder hereby irrevocably appoints Company as Founder's attorney-in-fact, coupled with an interest, to execute such documents on Founder's behalf.

## 7. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

Founder represents and warrants to Company that:

(a) Founder has the full right, power, and authority to enter into this Agreement and to make the assignments made herein;
(b) the Pre-Existing IP does not infringe the rights of any third party and is not subject to any prior assignment, license, or encumbrance;
(c) Founder has not entered into any agreement that would conflict with this Agreement or would impair Founder's ability to assign the Pre-Existing IP;
(d) Founder has listed all Excluded IP on Exhibit D (or attests that there is no Excluded IP).

## 8. CONFIDENTIALITY

Founder agrees that all Assigned IP and Pre-Existing IP contains Company's Confidential Information and shall not be disclosed by Founder to any third party without Company's prior written consent. This obligation continues after the termination of Founder's engagement with Company.

## 9. NO RIGHT TO USE NAME / LIKENESS

Nothing in this Agreement grants Founder any right to use Company's name, marks, or likeness, other than as Founder's role in the Business requires.

## 10. TERMINATION OF ENGAGEMENT

This Agreement survives the termination of Founder's employment, engagement, or role at Company. The assignments herein are perpetual and irrevocable.

## 11. MISCELLANEOUS

- **Entire agreement.** This Agreement, together with Exhibits A–D, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties on the subject matter and supersedes all prior discussions.
- **Amendments.** Only in writing signed by both Parties.
- **Governing law.** Delaware law, without regard to conflict of laws.
- **Jurisdiction.** Exclusive jurisdiction of Delaware state and federal courts.
- **Severability.** If any provision is unenforceable, the remaining provisions continue in full force.
- **Counterparts.** May be executed in counterparts, each deemed an original. Electronic signatures deemed originals.
- **No waiver.** Failure to enforce any provision is not a waiver.
- **Headings.** For convenience only; do not affect interpretation.
