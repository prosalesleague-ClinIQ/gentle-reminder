# Pre-Entity Outreach Safety Guide

**Status:** ACTIVE — Delaware C-Corp formation target: within 1 week. This guide governs outreach until the entity exists.
**Author:** Christo Mac, Founder
**Last updated:** 2026-04-20

---

## The one-sentence rule

**Until the Delaware C-Corp is formed, you may introduce the opportunity, share public-safe materials, request quotes, and schedule calls — but you may NOT sign anything, take money, or share trade-secret parameters.**

---

## Week 0 (now): what IS safe

| Action | Safe? | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Send a cold intro email referencing the pitch site | ✅ | No commitment created by an email. |
| Share public pitch site URL | ✅ | `https://gentle-reminder-pitch.vercel.app` — designed public-safe. |
| Share the public exec summary PDF | ✅ | `Gentle-Reminder-Exec-Summary.pdf` — no trade secrets. |
| Share the 16-slide pitch deck PDF | ✅ | `Gentle-Reminder-Pitch-Deck.pdf` — high-level metrics only, no claim language. |
| Share the public IP portfolio (`/ip`) | ✅ | Names + counts + tier, no full claim language, no trade-secret parameters. |
| Schedule a 30-minute exploratory call | ✅ | Calls are not legally binding. |
| Answer questions that don't require revealing specific numerical thresholds, weights, dictionaries, or unfiled inventions | ✅ | Stay at the "what it does" level, not the "how the numbers are tuned" level. |
| Request a written fee quote from a patent attorney | ✅ | A quote is not an engagement. |
| Request a written fee quote from a placement agent or CFO | ✅ | Same. |
| Ask a VC analyst what diligence they'd want once the entity forms | ✅ | Preparation, not commitment. |

## Week 0 (now): what is NOT safe

| Action | Safe? | Why |
|--------|-------|-----|
| Sign an NDA as an individual | ❌ | Creates personal liability. Assign to entity after formation instead. |
| Sign a patent-attorney engagement letter | ❌ | Same reason. Contingency / equity engagements especially — you cannot grant equity in a company that doesn't exist. |
| Sign a placement-agent engagement letter | ❌ | Same reason + unregistered brokers are illegal anyway (verify FINRA first). |
| Accept a term sheet | ❌ | Same reason. |
| Open a business bank account | ❌ | Requires EIN, which requires the entity. |
| Share full provisional patent specifications | ❌ | Wait for NDA execution, which waits for entity. |
| Share trade-secret parameters (specific weights, thresholds, dictionaries, calibration tables) | ❌ | These are retained separately precisely so they're NEVER casually shared. |
| Publicly disclose any unfiled invention (blog post, conference talk, published paper, LinkedIn post) | ❌ | Starts the 12-month US clock and destroys foreign patent rights. |
| Represent yourself as "Gentle Reminder, Inc." | ❌ | The corporation does not exist yet. Say "Gentle Reminder (Delaware C-Corp formation in final stages)" instead. |

## The disclosure line to include in every outreach email this week

Add this to the intro paragraph or the signature block:

> *Note on entity status: We're finalizing our Delaware C-Corp formation this week. Any NDA or engagement letter will execute under the incorporated entity once filed — anticipated within 7 days. Happy to schedule exploratory calls, share public materials, and exchange written fee quotes in the interim.*

This protects **both sides** — the counterparty knows why you're delaying signing, and you've documented the delay is intentional, not evasive.

## How to talk about the entity in real-time

- ❌ "I'm the CEO of Gentle Reminder, Inc."
- ✅ "I'm the founder of Gentle Reminder. We're finalizing the Delaware C-Corp formation this week."

- ❌ "Our company is incorporated in Delaware."
- ✅ "The Delaware C-Corp will be formed this week — we're mid-filing."

- ❌ "We'd love to sign the NDA today."
- ✅ "I'd like to park the NDA until my incorporation clears next week. Happy to send a redline this week so your counsel can review the terms."

## What to do if someone pushes to sign this week

- **Patent attorney:** "I understand — a few of the candidates I'm talking to are exploring equity arrangements. The cleanest path is for us to execute engagement letters against the corp rather than me personally. Can we schedule the signing call for [date + 7 days]? In the interim I can send you a written scope + fee summary so nothing is waiting on execution."
- **VC pushing for term sheet:** Highly unlikely pre-entity, but if: "Appreciate the momentum. Let's target term sheet discussions the week of [date + 7 days] so we can execute against the DE C-Corp directly — cleanest for 83(b), cap table, and the lead's legal team."
- **Anyone pushing an NDA today:** "Here's a redline of our mutual NDA [link] for your counsel to review. We'll execute once my entity formation clears — targeting [date]. Happy to operate under good-faith verbal confidentiality in the meantime, or to share only materials that are already on the public pitch site."

## Post-entity checklist (to run the day the DE C-Corp forms)

- [ ] Update all NDA templates to remove "(to be formed)" qualifiers
- [ ] Update email signatures with legal entity name + state of incorporation
- [ ] Open business bank account (Mercury / Brex / SVB)
- [ ] File EIN with IRS (usually same-day via IRS online)
- [ ] Sign Invention Assignment Agreement as founder (assign all pre-entity IP to the entity — CRITICAL; without this the entity doesn't own the patents)
- [ ] Re-sign / replace any NDAs signed personally pre-entity (you shouldn't have any; if any slipped, cure them)
- [ ] File 83(b) election within 30 days of founder stock issuance
- [ ] File trademark application for "Gentle Reminder" (see `trademark-filing-checklist.md`)
- [ ] Update `/private/checklists` — mark `entity-exists`, `ein-ready` manually
- [ ] Send a note to any active outreach: "Entity formation complete. Ready to execute NDA / engagement letter."
