## ARTICLE I — OFFICES

### Section 1.1 — Registered Office

The registered office of the Corporation in the State of Delaware shall be as set forth in the Certificate of Incorporation or as subsequently changed.

### Section 1.2 — Other Offices

The Corporation may have offices at such other places, within or outside the State of Delaware, as the Board of Directors may designate or as the business of the Corporation may require.

## ARTICLE II — STOCKHOLDERS

### Section 2.1 — Annual Meeting

The annual meeting of stockholders shall be held each year on a date and at a time and place designated by the Board of Directors, for the purpose of electing directors and for transacting such other business as may properly come before the meeting.

### Section 2.2 — Special Meetings

Special meetings of stockholders may be called by (a) the Board of Directors, (b) the Chief Executive Officer, or (c) the holders of not less than 25% of the voting power of the outstanding stock entitled to vote.

### Section 2.3 — Notice

Written notice of each meeting of stockholders, stating the place (if any), date, and time of such meeting and, in the case of a special meeting, the purpose for which the meeting is called, shall be given to each stockholder of record entitled to vote at such meeting not less than ten (10) nor more than sixty (60) days before the meeting date.

### Section 2.4 — Quorum

At all meetings of stockholders, a majority of the voting power of the issued and outstanding stock of the Corporation entitled to vote at such meeting, present in person or represented by proxy, shall constitute a quorum.

### Section 2.5 — Voting

Except as otherwise required by law, the Certificate of Incorporation, or these Bylaws:
- Each stockholder shall be entitled to one vote per share on each matter submitted to a vote of stockholders.
- Action shall be authorized by a majority of the voting power present in person or represented by proxy at a meeting at which a quorum is present.
- Directors shall be elected by a plurality of the votes cast.

### Section 2.6 — Action Without Meeting

Any action required or permitted to be taken at a meeting of stockholders may be taken without a meeting if a written consent setting forth the action is signed by the holders of outstanding stock having not less than the minimum number of votes that would be necessary to authorize or take such action at a meeting at which all shares entitled to vote thereon were present and voted.

### Section 2.7 — Proxies

Each stockholder entitled to vote at a meeting of stockholders may authorize another person or persons to act for such stockholder by proxy, executed in writing or transmitted in a manner permitted by applicable law.

## ARTICLE III — BOARD OF DIRECTORS

### Section 3.1 — Number and Term

The Board of Directors shall consist of one or more directors, as fixed from time to time by resolution of the Board or the stockholders. Each director shall hold office until the next annual meeting of stockholders and until such director's successor is elected and qualified, or until such director's earlier resignation, removal, or death.

### Section 3.2 — Initial Board

Until the first annual meeting of stockholders, the Board shall consist of the individuals named in the Action by Sole Incorporator (FORM-003).

### Section 3.3 — Vacancies

Any vacancy on the Board of Directors, including a vacancy resulting from an increase in the authorized number of directors, may be filled by a majority of the remaining directors or by the stockholders.

### Section 3.4 — Regular Meetings

Regular meetings of the Board of Directors may be held without notice at such times and places as the Board may determine from time to time.

### Section 3.5 — Special Meetings

Special meetings of the Board may be called by the Chair, the Chief Executive Officer, or any director, on at least twenty-four (24) hours' notice to each director.

### Section 3.6 — Quorum and Voting

A majority of the directors then in office shall constitute a quorum. The act of a majority of the directors present at a meeting at which a quorum is present shall be the act of the Board of Directors.

### Section 3.7 — Action Without Meeting

Any action required or permitted to be taken at a meeting of the Board may be taken without a meeting if all directors consent in writing (including by electronic transmission) to such action.

### Section 3.8 — Committees

The Board may designate one or more committees, each consisting of one or more directors, with such power and authority as may be provided by resolution of the Board, subject to the limitations of DE Gen. Corp. Law § 141(c).

### Section 3.9 — Compensation

Directors may receive compensation for their services as directors and reimbursement for their expenses, in such amounts as the Board may determine.

## ARTICLE IV — OFFICERS

### Section 4.1 — Officers

The officers of the Corporation shall include a Chief Executive Officer (or President), and a Secretary, and may include such other officers (including Chief Financial Officer, Treasurer, one or more Vice Presidents, and Assistant Officers) as the Board of Directors may from time to time appoint. Any two or more offices may be held by the same person.

### Section 4.2 — Appointment

The officers shall be appointed annually by the Board of Directors and shall hold office at the pleasure of the Board.

### Section 4.3 — Removal

Any officer may be removed, with or without cause, at any time by the Board of Directors. Such removal shall not prejudice any contract rights of the officer.

### Section 4.4 — Duties

Officers shall have such duties and authority as are customarily associated with their respective offices and such additional duties and authority as may be assigned by the Board of Directors.

## ARTICLE V — STOCK

### Section 5.1 — Certificates

Shares of the Corporation's stock may be certificated or uncertificated at the discretion of the Board. Certificates, when issued, shall be signed by the Chief Executive Officer (or President) and the Secretary (or an Assistant Secretary) and shall state the holder's name, the number and class of shares represented, and any required legends regarding restrictions on transfer.

### Section 5.2 — Transfer

Shares shall be transferable on the books of the Corporation upon surrender of the certificate (if certificated) and compliance with any applicable restrictions in the Certificate of Incorporation, these Bylaws, or any agreement to which the holder is a party.

### Section 5.3 — Lost Certificates

The Corporation may issue a replacement certificate in place of a lost, stolen, or destroyed certificate upon such terms as the Board may require, which may include a bond of indemnity.

### Section 5.4 — Record Date

The Board may fix a record date, not more than sixty (60) nor less than ten (10) days before the date of any meeting of stockholders, for the purpose of determining the stockholders entitled to notice of and to vote at such meeting.

## ARTICLE VI — INDEMNIFICATION

### Section 6.1 — Scope

The Corporation shall indemnify each director and officer of the Corporation, and may indemnify each employee and agent of the Corporation, to the fullest extent permitted by DE Gen. Corp. Law § 145, as set forth in the Certificate of Incorporation.

### Section 6.2 — Advancement of Expenses

The Corporation shall advance expenses (including attorneys' fees) incurred by an indemnitee in defending any proceeding, as set forth in the Certificate of Incorporation.

### Section 6.3 — Insurance

The Corporation may purchase and maintain insurance on behalf of any indemnitee against any liability asserted against or incurred by such indemnitee in any such capacity.

## ARTICLE VII — MISCELLANEOUS

### Section 7.1 — Fiscal Year

The fiscal year of the Corporation shall be the calendar year unless otherwise fixed by resolution of the Board of Directors.

### Section 7.2 — Corporate Seal

The Corporation may, but need not, have a corporate seal. If a corporate seal is adopted, it shall be in such form as the Board of Directors may approve.

### Section 7.3 — Checks and Drafts

All checks, drafts, and other orders for the payment of money issued in the name of the Corporation shall be signed by such persons as the Board of Directors may from time to time designate.

### Section 7.4 — Notices

Except as otherwise required by law, the Certificate of Incorporation, or these Bylaws, notices may be given by hand delivery, by first-class mail, or by electronic transmission.

### Section 7.5 — Waiver of Notice

A written waiver of notice, signed by the person entitled to notice (whether before or after the time of the event to which such notice relates), shall be deemed equivalent to notice.

### Section 7.6 — Conflict with Certificate of Incorporation

If any provision of these Bylaws conflicts with any provision of the Certificate of Incorporation, the Certificate of Incorporation shall govern.

## ARTICLE VIII — AMENDMENT

These Bylaws may be amended, altered, or repealed (a) by the affirmative vote of a majority of the Board of Directors, or (b) by the affirmative vote of the holders of a majority of the voting power of the outstanding shares of stock entitled to vote.
