## What this document does (plain English)

This is the first "board meeting" of Gentle Reminder Corp — held as a written consent (no actual meeting required under DE GCL § 141(f)). It authorizes the 15 key things that turn the corp from a legal shell into a functioning business:

1. Ratifies the incorporator's actions so no future dispute can unwind what you did
2. Elects officers (all you, at formation)
3. Sets the principal office
4. Authorizes the founder stock issuance (5M shares to you for $500)
5. Authorizes the 83(b) election (CRITICAL — don't miss the 30-day window)
6. Reserves 1.5M shares for the option pool
7. Authorizes EIN application, bank accounts, and professional engagements
8. Authorizes the FIAA — transfers your pre-formation IP to the corp
9. Authorizes the trademark filing

Everything you do later as CEO derives from this document. Without it, you have a corp but no one is authorized to act on its behalf.

## Compliance notes

- DE Gen. Corp. Law § 141(f) — Board action by written consent permitted without meeting.
- DE GCL § 157 — Authorization of stock issuance by Board.
- DE GCL § 109 — Bylaw adoption by Board (already done by incorporator; ratification here).
- 26 U.S.C. § 83(b) — Federal tax election, 30-day deadline starts at stock transfer.
- Section 7 language mirrors IRS guidance on 83(b) authorization.
- Section 12 FIAA tied to `docs/legal/ip-assignment-agreement-template.md` (FIAA-001) — critical for pre-formation IP capture.
