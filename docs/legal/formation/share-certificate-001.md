## Filing / retention instructions

1. **Print the certificate** on quality stock paper (8.5 × 11" works; archival-grade preferred). Or keep as a signed PDF in corporate records — both are valid under DE GCL § 158.
2. **Fill in the Effective Date + Issue Date** — same as FSPA (FORM-005) signing date.
3. **Sign as CEO and as Secretary** — you hold both offices per Initial Board Resolutions (FORM-004).
4. **Deliver a signed copy to the Purchaser** (yourself). Retain a counter-copy in corporate records.
5. **Record the issuance** in the corporation's stock transfer ledger (create a simple spreadsheet or use Carta / Pulley once you set those up).
6. **Save as** `docs/legal/formation/share-certificate-001-signed.pdf`.

## What this document does (plain English)

This is the physical (or digital) evidence that you own 5,000,000 shares of Gentle Reminder Corp. It's to stock what a deed is to land.

Under modern DE law, a corp can choose to have certificated OR uncertificated (book-entry) stock. Either is legally sufficient. For pre-seed simplicity, a single paper/PDF certificate per stockholder is conventional and clear.

The two legends at the bottom remind any future holder that:
1. These shares can't be freely sold — federal/state securities laws apply
2. These shares are subject to the FSPA — vesting, repurchase, ROFR, lockup

Once the Corp is set up on Carta / Pulley (post-seed close), certificates are usually retired in favor of electronic book-entry.

## Compliance notes

- DE Gen. Corp. Law § 158 permits both certificated and uncertificated stock.
- Legends required under Rule 144 (Securities Act) and FSPA cross-reference.
- Double-officer signature (CEO + Secretary) is a tradition, not a statutory requirement — DE allows single-officer signature if Bylaws specify. Both roles are you, so it's decorative.
