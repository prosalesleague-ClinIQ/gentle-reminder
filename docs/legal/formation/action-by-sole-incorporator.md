## What this document does (plain English)

This is the procedural handoff from you-as-incorporator to you-as-director. When you filed the Cert of Incorporation, you were the "incorporator" — a one-time role whose only job is to file the Cert. The incorporator has the power to do three things and only three things:

1. **Adopt Bylaws** — the corp now has governing rules
2. **Appoint the first Board of Directors** — you name yourself as sole director
3. **Resign** — incorporator role terminates

Once signed, the corp has a board. The board (which is you) will then adopt Initial Board Resolutions (FORM-004) to elect officers, authorize stock issuance, etc.

## Compliance notes

- DE Gen. Corp. Law § 107 empowers the incorporator to adopt bylaws and elect directors prior to the election of directors by stockholders.
- Once directors are elected here, the Board takes over governance; incorporator powers are exhausted.
- A single document combining all three actions is standard — no need for separate documents.
- Filing with DE is NOT required — this is an internal corporate record.
