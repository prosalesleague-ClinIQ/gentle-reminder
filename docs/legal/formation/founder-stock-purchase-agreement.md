## 2. VESTING

### 2.1 Vesting Schedule

The Shares shall vest over a four (4)-year period with a one (1)-year cliff, measured from the **Vesting Commencement Date**, as follows:

- **Vesting Commencement Date:** [DATE CERTIFICATE OF INCORPORATION WAS FILED WITH DELAWARE]
- **Cliff:** 25% of the Shares (1,250,000 shares) vest on the first anniversary of the Vesting Commencement Date
- **Monthly thereafter:** 1/48th of the Shares (approximately 104,166 shares) vest on each monthly anniversary of the Vesting Commencement Date for the following 36 months
- **Full vesting:** 100% of the Shares vested on the fourth anniversary of the Vesting Commencement Date

### 2.2 Acceleration on Change of Control

*Intentionally omitted.* Acceleration on change of control is typically negotiated at seed or Series A via separate acceleration agreements. This clean baseline preserves optionality.

### 2.3 Continuous Service Required

The Shares shall vest only if and to the extent the Purchaser has continuously provided services to the Corporation (as an employee, officer, director, consultant, or advisor) from the Effective Date through the applicable vesting date. "Continuous service" is not broken by a bona fide leave of absence approved by the Corporation.

## 3. REPURCHASE OPTION

### 3.1 Repurchase Right

In the event that the Purchaser's continuous service to the Corporation terminates for any reason (including resignation, termination, disability, or death), the Corporation shall have the right and option (the "**Repurchase Right**") to purchase all or any portion of the **unvested Shares** at the original purchase price of $0.0001 per share.

### 3.2 Exercise of Repurchase Right

The Corporation may exercise the Repurchase Right by written notice to the Purchaser (or Purchaser's estate) delivered within **ninety (90) days** after the termination of continuous service. Closing of the repurchase shall occur within thirty (30) days after delivery of such notice, upon payment of the Repurchase Price in cash or cancellation of any indebtedness owed by the Purchaser to the Corporation.

### 3.3 Expiration

The Repurchase Right shall terminate as to Shares that have vested pursuant to Section 2.1. Upon full vesting of all Shares, the Repurchase Right shall terminate entirely.

### 3.4 Assignment

The Corporation may assign the Repurchase Right to any third party, subject to applicable securities laws.

## 4. TRANSFER RESTRICTIONS

### 4.1 General Restriction

The Purchaser shall not sell, transfer, pledge, hypothecate, or otherwise dispose of (collectively, "**Transfer**") any Shares except in accordance with this Agreement, the Bylaws, and applicable securities laws.

### 4.2 Right of First Refusal

Before any Transfer of Shares by the Purchaser (other than a Permitted Transfer under Section 4.5), the Purchaser shall give the Corporation written notice (the "**Transfer Notice**") containing: (i) the identity of the proposed transferee, (ii) the number of Shares proposed to be transferred, (iii) the proposed purchase price, (iv) the other terms of the proposed Transfer.

The Corporation shall have thirty (30) days after receipt of the Transfer Notice to elect, by written notice to the Purchaser, to purchase all (but not less than all) of the Shares described in the Transfer Notice at the price and on the terms set forth therein.

### 4.3 Co-Sale Right

*Reserved.* Co-sale rights are typically granted to investors at the seed round via a separate Right of First Refusal and Co-Sale Agreement.

### 4.4 Lockup — Initial Public Offering

The Purchaser agrees that in connection with the Corporation's initial public offering ("**IPO**") of its equity securities, the Purchaser shall not, for a period of up to **180 days** following the effective date of the registration statement, offer, sell, contract to sell, pledge, or otherwise dispose of any Shares (or any securities convertible into, exercisable for, or exchangeable for Shares), or enter into any swap or similar arrangement that transfers any of the economic consequences of ownership of the Shares. The Purchaser shall execute such customary lock-up agreements as the underwriters of the IPO may reasonably request.

### 4.5 Permitted Transfers

Notwithstanding Section 4.1, the Purchaser may Transfer Shares (i) to the Purchaser's immediate family (spouse, children, parents, siblings) or to a trust established for the benefit of such family members, (ii) by testamentary disposition, or (iii) with the Corporation's prior written consent. Any transferee pursuant to this Section 4.5 shall, as a condition of such Transfer, execute a counterpart to this Agreement and be bound by all of its terms.

## 5. SECTION 83(b) ELECTION

### 5.1 Purchaser's Right to File

The Purchaser acknowledges and understands that the Shares are subject to a substantial risk of forfeiture (via the Repurchase Right in Section 3), and therefore the Purchaser may file an election under Section 83(b) of the Internal Revenue Code (the "**83(b) Election**") to include the fair market value of the Shares (less the Purchase Price) in gross income for the taxable year in which the Shares are transferred.

### 5.2 Purchaser Responsibility

**The 83(b) Election must be filed with the Internal Revenue Service within thirty (30) days after the Effective Date.** Filing the 83(b) Election is the sole responsibility of the Purchaser, not the Corporation. The Purchaser should consult a tax advisor regarding the 83(b) Election and its consequences.

The Purchaser is encouraged to use the form of 83(b) Election in **Exhibit A** to this Agreement and to mail it via **USPS Certified Mail with Return Receipt Requested** to the IRS Service Center that processes the Purchaser's individual Form 1040.

### 5.3 Notice to Corporation

The Purchaser shall provide the Corporation with a copy of any filed 83(b) Election within seven (7) days after filing.

## What this document does (plain English)

This is the actual sale: you buy 5,000,000 shares of Gentle Reminder Health Corp for $500. The shares vest over 4 years with a 1-year cliff, meaning:
- If you quit or are terminated within the first 12 months, the corp can buy back ALL your shares for $500 (you'd get your money back, no equity)
- After month 12, 25% is yours permanently; the rest vests monthly over 36 more months
- Full vesting at month 48

**Why do founders put themselves on a vesting schedule?** Because Series A investors insist on it. If you have unvested shares and haven't filed 83(b), a future investor will require you to enter a vesting agreement AND will charge you ordinary-income tax on the already-appreciated value. Setting it now at $0 difference = zero tax hit. This is textbook.

**Why the Corp gets a repurchase right:** Protects the other founders and the corp — if someone leaves early with 100% of their equity, they're a "dead weight" stockholder. The repurchase right ensures equity tracks contribution.

**Why the 83(b) is critical:** Without 83(b), every vesting event triggers ordinary-income tax on the spread between purchase price ($500) and then-current FMV. By year 3, FMV could be $10M+ (if raising a Series A). Filing 83(b) NOW locks in a $0 tax event today and converts all future appreciation to capital gains. Missing the 30-day deadline costs founders hundreds of thousands to millions in ordinary-income tax over their career.

## Compliance notes

- Vesting schedule (§2.1) is the industry standard pre-seed (4-year / 1-year cliff / monthly).
- Repurchase Right (§3) gives Corp the right but not the obligation — balanced.
- ROFR (§4.2) gives Corp first crack at any transfer — standard.
- Lock-up language (§4.4) pre-commits to IPO lockup — underwriter-friendly.
- Section 83(b) language (§5) puts filing responsibility squarely on Purchaser — Corp not liable for missed filings.
- Securities law reps (§6.2, §6.3, §6.5) invoke the Rule 506(b) private placement exemption for issuances to accredited investors — standard Regulation D path.
