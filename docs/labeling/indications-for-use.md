# Indications for Use

**Document ID:** IFU-001
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** 2026-04-06
**Author:** Regulatory Affairs

---

## 1. Device Name

**Gentle Reminder** Cognitive Health Monitoring Platform

## 2. Intended Use

Gentle Reminder is a software-based cognitive health monitoring platform intended to provide supplementary cognitive assessment data to healthcare providers for use in the ongoing monitoring of adults aged 65 and older who have been clinically diagnosed with mild-to-moderate dementia.

The device is intended to:

1. **Monitor cognitive function** across six domains (orientation, memory, attention, language, visuospatial, executive function) through interactive exercises administered on a mobile device or web browser
2. **Track longitudinal trends** in cognitive performance to assist clinicians in identifying changes over time
3. **Deliver medication reminders** and track adherence for prescribed medications
4. **Alert designated caregivers** to significant changes in cognitive scores, missed medications, and detected fall events
5. **Exchange clinical data** with Electronic Health Record (EHR) systems via FHIR R4 standard

## 3. Intended Patient Population

- Adults aged 65 years and older
- Clinically diagnosed with mild-to-moderate dementia (MMSE score 10-26 or equivalent)
- Able to interact with a touchscreen device, with or without caregiver assistance
- Living independently, in assisted living, or in a care facility

## 4. Intended Users

| User | Role |
|------|------|
| **Patients** | Complete cognitive exercises, receive medication reminders, interact with the platform daily |
| **Caregivers** (family or professional) | Receive alerts, monitor patient cognitive trends, manage medication schedules |
| **Clinicians** (physicians, neuropsychologists, nurses) | Review longitudinal cognitive data, assess trends, inform clinical decisions |
| **Healthcare administrators** | Manage organization accounts, configure system settings |

## 5. Intended Use Environment

- Home setting (patient's residence)
- Assisted living facilities
- Memory care units
- Outpatient clinical settings (for clinician review)
- Any setting with internet connectivity for data synchronization

## 6. Contraindications

Gentle Reminder should NOT be used:

1. As a standalone diagnostic tool for dementia or any other neurological condition
2. As a replacement for standardized neuropsychological assessment (MMSE, MoCA, or equivalent)
3. As the sole basis for clinical treatment decisions
4. For patients with severe dementia who cannot interact with a touchscreen device even with caregiver assistance
5. As a primary fall detection system or life-safety device

## 7. Warnings

1. **Not a diagnostic device.** Cognitive scores generated by Gentle Reminder are supplementary clinical information only. All diagnostic and treatment decisions must be made by qualified healthcare professionals based on comprehensive clinical assessment.

2. **Supplementary monitoring only.** Gentle Reminder does not replace regular clinical visits, standardized cognitive assessments, or professional caregiver supervision.

3. **Fall detection limitations.** Fall detection relies on wearable device sensors and may not detect all falls. Falls may go undetected if the wearable device is not worn, is malfunctioning, or the fall pattern is atypical. Do not rely on this system as a primary fall detection mechanism.

4. **Medication reminders are supplementary.** Gentle Reminder medication reminders supplement, but do not replace, clinical medication management. Medication schedules must be prescribed and managed by qualified healthcare providers.

5. **Alert delivery depends on connectivity.** Alert delivery to caregivers requires network connectivity. Alerts may be delayed or undeliverable during network outages or when the caregiver's device is unreachable.

6. **Score variability.** Cognitive scores may vary based on factors unrelated to cognitive function, including fatigue, mood, medication effects, time of day, and environmental distractions. Clinicians should interpret scores in the context of the whole clinical picture.

7. **Cultural and linguistic considerations.** Exercise content is available in 8 languages but may contain cultural references that affect performance in certain populations. Clinicians should consider cultural context when interpreting scores.

## 8. Precautions

1. Ensure the patient's device has adequate battery and network connectivity for exercise sessions and alert delivery.
2. Verify that caregiver contact information is current and that alert preferences are correctly configured.
3. Review medication reminder schedules regularly with the prescribing clinician.
4. Periodically verify that wearable device sensors are functioning correctly for fall detection features.
5. Ensure the patient's device is protected with biometric or passcode security to prevent unauthorized access to health information.

## 9. Prescription Use

Gentle Reminder is intended for use under the supervision of a qualified healthcare professional. Initial setup and configuration of clinical parameters should be performed or approved by the patient's clinician.
