# IP Portfolio Valuation

## Valuation Methodologies

Three standard approaches to IP valuation (per AICPA guidelines):

### 1. Cost Approach
Replacement or reproduction cost.

**Our platform cost approximation:**
- 53,000+ lines of production code
- Industry average cost: $40-$80 per line of clinical-grade code (includes testing, documentation, regulatory compliance)
- **Estimated reproduction cost: $2.1M - $4.2M**

This is the FLOOR. Not suitable for standalone IP valuation but establishes a baseline.

### 2. Market Approach
Comparable transactions.

**Comparable transactions (publicly available):**

| Transaction | Approx. Value | Notes |
|-------------|--------------:|-------|
| Pear Therapeutics (pre-bankruptcy) | $1.6B peak / $0 post | DTx platform. Overvalued. |
| Akili Interactive (2022 IPO) | $1B peak / ~$25M post | EndeavorRx (ADHD DTx). Overvalued. |
| Cognoa Series C (2021) | $100M-$150M post | Autism diagnostic DTx. |
| Linus Health Series B (2024) | $55M raise / ~$250M-$400M valuation | Digital cognitive assessment. **Most comparable.** |
| Biofourmis Series D (2022) | $300M raise / ~$1.3B valuation | Digital biomarkers. Highly valued. |
| BrainCheck (2021) | $10M raise | Cognitive assessment. Small scale. |

**Key lessons:**
- Linus Health at ~$250-400M valuation with similar platform scope is our most direct comp
- Cognoa at $100-150M for narrower diagnostic focus
- Akili/Pear post-IPO collapse teaches that valuation must be tied to proven revenue traction

**Adjusted valuation range (pre-revenue, 23-patent portfolio, FDA SaMD pathway):** $30M-$80M

### 3. Income Approach
DCF of expected cash flows.

**Projected royalty streams (selected IP licensing to 3 strategics):**

| 3 | $2M royalty | $500K milestone | $250K upfront | $2.75M |

- Years 6-20 NPV: ~$25M

### Weighted Portfolio Valuation

**Pre-filing (current state):** $8M - $35M
- Provisional patents provide priority but no issued rights
- Discount for uncertainty

**Post-provisional filing:** $15M - $50M
- Priority dates secured
- Partial legal protection

**Post-non-provisional (year 2):** $25M - $80M
- Non-provisionals in prosecution
- USPTO examination status known

**Post-grant + post-FDA clearance:** $50M - $200M
- Enforceable patents
- Market entry validated

## Valuation Drivers

### Factors that INCREASE valuation:
- ✅ FDA 510(k) clearance
- ✅ Clinical validation data (peer-reviewed)
- ✅ Customer traction (facility contracts)
- ✅ Strategic partner interest (LOI or term sheet)
- ✅ Patent issuance (USPTO grant)
- ✅ International coverage (PCT, national phase)
- ✅ Revenue/ARR growth

### Factors that DECREASE valuation:
- ❌ Prior art emergence blocking claims
- ❌ FDA setbacks (NOI, request for additional data)
- ❌ Competitor patents with earlier filing dates
- ❌ Key inventor departure without proper IP assignment
- ❌ Delayed non-provisional conversion (priority loss)
- ❌ Claim narrowing during prosecution

## Funding Round Implications

### Seed ($5M at $25M post-money)
- Assumes IP portfolio at $8M-$15M + platform/team/traction at $10M-$17M
- 20% dilution reasonable

### Series A ($15-25M at $75-100M post-money)
- Requires IP portfolio grown to $25M-$40M via:
  - Non-provisional conversions on top 7-10 IPs
  - Clinical validation data
  - 3-5 pilot customer contracts

### Series B ($30-50M at $150-250M post-money)
- Requires:
  - FDA 510(k) clearance or De Novo
  - Commercial traction ($5M+ ARR)
  - Issued US patents (at least 2-3)
  - International filings underway

## Exit Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Strategic Acquisition (Year 3-4)
- Acquirer: Biogen / Eisai / Lilly / UnitedHealth
- Valuation: $150M - $500M
- Driver: FDA-cleared DTx + issued patents + commercial traction

### Scenario 2: PE Rollup (Year 4-5)
- Acquirer: Healthcare-focused PE (Apollo, TPG Sixth Street, KKR Health)
- Valuation: $100M - $300M
- Driver: EBITDA-positive SaaS + IP licensing

### Scenario 3: IPO (Year 5-7)
- Valuation at IPO: $500M - $2B
- Requires: $30M+ ARR, proven market fit, mature clinical evidence
- Risk: Akili/Pear post-IPO collapses show DTx IPOs are volatile

### Scenario 4: IP-Only License/Sale
- Sell IP portfolio to strategic, wind down operating company
- Valuation: $20M - $80M
- Downside scenario if commercial traction doesn't materialize

## Due Diligence Readiness

For any valuation discussion, prepare:

- [ ] All 23 patent drafts with prior art analysis
- [ ] Patent attorney opinion on enforceability
- [ ] Invention assignment agreements from all inventors
- [ ] FTO (Freedom to Operate) opinion
- [ ] Competitor patent landscape analysis
- [ ] Revenue model with unit economics
- [ ] Clinical validation data (as it accumulates)
- [ ] Customer pipeline with contract stages
- [ ] Regulatory filings and FDA correspondence
- [ ] Financial statements and cap table

## Disclosure

These valuation figures are estimates based on:
- Public comparable transactions
- Industry rules of thumb
- Management projections

They are NOT a formal 409A valuation or financial opinion. For a binding valuation (e.g., for tax, M&A, or litigation), engage a qualified third-party valuation firm (recommended: Kroll, Duff & Phelps, Houlihan Lokey, or boutique IP valuation specialists like Consor or IP Metrics).
