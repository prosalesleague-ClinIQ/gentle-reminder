# Patent Attorneys & Capital-Raising Partners — Contingent/Equity Compensation

**CONFIDENTIAL**

This document identifies patent attorneys who accept deferred fees or equity, and capital-raising advisors/placement agents who work on success-fee or equity basis for medtech/AI healthcare startups.

---

## PART A — PATENT ATTORNEYS (Deferred Fees / Equity)

### Ethical & Legal Context
Patent attorneys can accept equity or contingency fees under ABA Model Rule 1.8(a), provided:
1. Terms are fair, reasonable, and in writing
2. Client is given opportunity to consult independent counsel
3. Client consents in writing
4. Many jurisdictions restrict contingency for patent prosecution (not litigation) — verify with each candidate

1. **File Tier 1 (5 provisionals) with Carson Patents or Wojcik** on equity/contingency — fast, low-friction, test the model
2. **Engage WSGR or Cooley via warm intro** for the full portfolio + non-provisional conversion strategy

- Give more than 3-5% total equity to legal providers (aggregated)
- Accept verbal deferred-fee agreements
- Mix prosecution and litigation relationships without a clear engagement scope

## PART B — CAPITAL-RAISING ADVISORS & PLACEMENT AGENTS

### Compensation Reality Check

**Standard seed placement agent fees:**
- 3-5% cash commission for small institutional / angel networks or repeat trusted intermediaries
- 5-8% for independent placement agents covering broad investor pools
- 8-10%+ for hard-to-place deals or cross-border sourcing

**Equity compensation:**
- 0.25-2% equity for seed-stage capital
- Common combination: 3-7% cash + 0.5-1.5% equity

**Regulatory note:** Under SEC Rule 3a4-1 and FINRA rules, ONLY licensed broker-dealers can legally collect success fees on securities offerings. "Fundraising consultants" who are not broker-dealers collecting percentage-of-capital fees violate federal securities law and can invalidate your round. Verify FINRA/SEC registration for anyone taking a success fee.

## RECOMMENDED EXECUTION PATH

### Week 1: Patent Attorney Triage
1. Send parallel inquiries to **Carson Patents**, **Miller IP Law**, **Wojcik Law Firm** for Tier 1 (5 patents) on equity/contingency
2. Request warm intro to **WSGR (Hin Au)** or **Cooley medtech team** via:
   - Columbia Tech Ventures attorney list contacts
   - Any healthcare VC you've already conversed with
   - Alumni networks (Stanford, MIT, etc.)
3. Compare proposals by: total cost, equity stake requested, filing timeline, conversion strategy for non-provisionals

### Week 2: Capital Partner Outreach
1. **For seed placement:** Contact **New Harbor Venture Partners** and **Locust Walk** — describe 23-patent portfolio + FDA SaMD pathway + $5M ask
2. **For fractional fundraising CFO:** Interview 3 candidates from **Burkland**, **airCFO**, **Kruze** — look for prior dementia/digital health experience
3. **For accelerators:** Apply to **Rock Health**, **MATTER**, **StartUp Health** (parallel applications)

### Week 3-4: Negotiate Compensation Structure

#### Patent attorney engagement letter must specify:
- Exact scope (per-patent fixed fee or hourly cap)
- Equity % (vested over filing completion, typically)
- Who pays USPTO fees (usually client pays directly)
- Conversion options (cash payment replacing equity at specified events)
- Termination terms (if founder wants to end relationship)

#### Capital raiser engagement must specify:
- **FINRA broker-dealer registration verification** (public: [finra.org/brokercheck](https://www.finra.org/brokercheck))
- Cash success fee % (3-8% typical)
- Equity / warrant grant (0.5-1.5% typical)
- Who counts as "introduced investor" (tail period for attribution)
- Exclusivity term (3-12 months typical)
- Reimbursed expenses (capped, with approval)

## RED FLAGS — AVOID THESE

### Patent Attorney Red Flags
- ❌ Requests more than 5% equity for a simple portfolio
- ❌ Unwilling to cap total equity if fees accumulate
- ❌ No written engagement letter
- ❌ Commits to outcomes ("I guarantee your patent will be granted")
- ❌ Not registered with USPTO (verify: [uspto.gov/patents/apply/using-legal-services/finding-patent-practitioner](https://www.uspto.gov/patents/apply/using-legal-services/finding-patent-practitioner))

### Capital Raiser Red Flags
- ❌ Charges success fee without broker-dealer registration (illegal; voids your round)
- ❌ Asks for upfront cash > $10K without clear deliverables
- ❌ Requests equity > 2% for introduction-only work
- ❌ Claims exclusive relationships with specific VCs (verifiable lie)
- ❌ Demands > 12 month exclusivity
- ❌ Tail period for attribution > 12 months post-termination
- ❌ Unwilling to define "introduced investor" precisely
- ❌ Focus on "crypto investors" or "family offices we can't name"

### Broader Red Flags
- ❌ Anyone who asks for money to "open doors" without specific named investors
- ❌ "I can connect you to [major VC] for a fee" — these intros are FREE via portfolio companies
- ❌ Pressure to sign quickly ("this deal closes by Friday")
- ❌ Unwillingness to provide references from past clients

## NOTE ON TIMING

Given your 23 provisional filings need to happen within 6 weeks (per `PATENT-FILING-ACTION-PLAN.md`), and seed fundraising typically takes 3-6 months, here is the recommended sequencing:

| Timing | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Weeks 1-2 | Engage patent attorneys (file Tier 1) — speed matters for priority dates |
| Weeks 3-4 | Complete all 23 filings |
| Weeks 4-8 | Engage fundraising advisor OR go direct to VCs |
| Months 3-5 | Close seed round |
| Month 6+ | With seed capital, retain top-tier IP firm for non-provisional conversions |

**Don't wait to engage a capital-raising advisor to start VC conversations.** Direct outreach to healthtech VCs is free, effective, and doesn't require an intermediary. Use advisors when you specifically need: (a) access to investors you cannot reach directly, (b) deal structuring expertise beyond founder capability, or (c) negotiation leverage.
